Welcome to Q Condos, the newest landmark in Downtown Kitchener, soaring 34 storeys above Queen Street’s creative hub. Designed to complement the city’s energy, Q Condos offers the perfect blend of modern design, smart technology, and urban convenience—all just steps from Victoria Park, ION rapid transit, top restaurants, entertainment, and the region’s thriving tech scene.

Built with Momentum, Designed for the Future

Q Condos joins the prestigious lineup of Downtown Kitchener and Uptown Waterloo developments by Momentum Developments, including Charlie West, Garment Street, One Victoria, and One Hundred Condominiums. For nearly two decades, Momentum Developments has been shaping the skyline of Waterloo Region, crafting thriving communities with exceptional quality, modern design, and a vision for the future.

Q Condos – Elevate Your Lifestyle in the Heart of Kitchener

Discover Q Condos, a striking 34-storey landmark in Downtown Kitchener, perfectly positioned in the city’s vibrant creative district. Offering a seamless blend of contemporary design, cutting-edge technology, and urban convenience, Q Condos places you steps away from Victoria Park, ION rapid transit, premier dining, entertainment, and the region’s booming tech industry. Developed by Momentum Developments—renowned for shaping Waterloo Region’s skyline—Q Condos continues their legacy of excellence, delivering modern living spaces designed for the future.

The past meets the present.

The original 1917 building at the corner of 20 Queen Street and Goudies Lane was inspired by the Beaux-Art architectural era. Q Condos' slender stance will rise high above this original reclaimed brick building with large window wall glazing, coloured concrete panels and perforated metal panel balcony screens.

Community benefit space planned.

The main floor of Q Condos will open into a new community space in Goudies Lane, featuring a boutique coffee shop, social space and performance area where patrons will enjoy scheduled art and music events.

Celebrating the history of music.

Queen Street and Goudies Lane was home to various conservatories of music and dance. To continue the creativity at this corner, a community performance space will be designed and built at the base of Q Condos.
